2  Municipal Overview

The Town of Amherst Municipality was responsible for 4764.7 Metric Tons of CO2 Equivalent emissions in FY 2016. In FY 2025, this number was 3342.5. This represents a 29.8% decrease in municipal emissions relative to the 2016 baseline, which exceeded the Town’s emissions reduction goal for 2025.

GHG emissions by year, along with percentage reductions relative to the 2016 baseline.
Inventory Year Total MTCo2e Percent Reduction
FY 2016 4,765 0%
FY 2022 4,142 -13%
FY 2025 3,342 -30%

Note: Previous inventories reported 4,389 and 3,852 MTCO2e for FY 2016 and FY 2022, respectively. These numbers were updated to reflect the latest data sources and methodologies.

Municipal emissions fall into two categories: stationary energy and transportation. Stationary energy accounted for 79.6% of total emissions in 2025, up from 66.1% in 2022. As it has been reported, electricity usage from electric vehicles (EVs) has been absorbed into stationary energy, so this increase in electricity consumption likely reflects the increased adoption of EVs and their miscategorization as stationary energy, rather than a reduction in transportation.

2.1 Emissions by Fuel Type

As shown in Figure 2.2, the composition of fuel usage has changed over time within the municipality. Most notably, electricity has seen an increased share of total emissions, from 36% in 2022 to 46% in 2025, while the relative shares of gasoline and diesel have decreased. This is consistent with the Town’s move toward more renewable energy sources.
